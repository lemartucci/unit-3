Agricultural Practices in New York State (2017)

Agricultural Practice Definitions

Alley Cropping relates to planting rows of trees within agricultural fields and planting crops between those rows. Silvopasture relates to incorporating trees and livestock grazing in the same pasture. Cover Cropping is the practice of growing non-cash crops to keep the soil covered in order to reduce erosion; these are typically planted between cash crop harvests. No tillage is the practice of not implementing soil tillage in order to minimize soil disruption and the detriments tillage can cause Rotational/Intensive Grazing Artifical Ditch Drainage Tile Drainage Conservation Easement
